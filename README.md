# Motivation
This paper questions the three propositions that have effectively functioned as practical axioms in deep learning and tries to deprive them of their axiomatic status. The first concerns activation functions, which have been used throughout the entire history of deep learning to introduce nonlinearity and are employed in nearly all models. However, nonlinearity can be sufficiently provided through alternative mechanisms, and I demonstrate that activation functions inside the model are not necessary. The second and third propositions concern residual connections and normalization layers, which have been used almost obligatorily for approximately the past decade. Following the study showing that ResNet achieved outstanding performance on ImageNet, residual connections have been widely adopted across almost all models. In addition, normalization layers are applied immediately after linear operations within ResNet, and numerous studies have shown that inserting normalization layers leads to successful improvements in accuracy in most architectures, making them effectively indispensable components. In this paper, I propose the Jongwoo Block, which uses no activation functions, no residual connections, or no normalization layers. Using networks composed of these blocks, which I call the Jongwoo network, I demonstrate performance superior to ResNet on CIFAR-10 and further show strong performance on ImageNet.
